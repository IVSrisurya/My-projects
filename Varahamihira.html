About

Sage Varahamihira or was a renowned Astronomer, Mathematician, and Astrologer . He was the renowned scientist, who discovered water on Mars, even before 1500 years. He hailed from Ujjain and is believed to have lived during the Gupta period. It was the golden period of Indian era, where several art, science, culture, architecture, science had developed, and was taught to various people from other parts of the world. Sage Varahamihira is one of the Nine Gems called “Navaratnas”, in the court of King Yashodharman.

He is considered as the pioneer in mathematics, and a tremendous scholar. He was one of the mathematicians- astrologers of India . Apart from Sanskrit, he also learned Greek and praised for their acumen in Astrology. His works are translated into several languages across the globe.

Life of Sage Varahamihira:

Sage Varahamihira was born in the Avanti region, which is in Malwa. He was born to Adityadasa, a great Brahmin scholar himself in both astrology, astronomy and mathematics. Adityadasa was a great worshipper of Sun, and he taught Sage Varahamihira in several fields and raised him as a scholar.it is also believed that, throughout his life, he had some quest, which made him discover new things. He met the great mathematician Aryabhata and took astronomy, and mathematics as his lifetime pursuit.

He had an open mind to even learn languages and science from other parts of the world, and make a comprehensive study. It is also evident from his works that he had intellectual connections with scholars of the other parts of the world.

Legend of Sage Varahamihira:

Sage Varahamihira predicted King Vikramaditya’s son’s death. consistent with him, the King’s son would die in his 18th age. Despite several protection, the son of the king was attacked by a boar . Thus, the king to commemorate his accurate predication, and to praise him, conferred him with the title “Varaha”, which is that the emblem of Mahada Kingdom. Since then Sage Mihira was called Varahamihira.

Surya Siddhanta

Before 1000 BC, people in India had begun to use astronomical instruments. During this point , the ‘Suryasidhanta', a well known book for astronomical calculations, was written. The word ‘Suryasidhanta' means ‘sun theory,' and it refers to the measurements of star and planet positions. Some Indian mathematicians later developed their own instruments and methods to assist within the understanding of the ‘Suryasidhanta' theory. One such invaluable contribution is that the introduction of zero in mathematics and therefore the decimal method of calculation. Varahamihira compared Surya Siddhanta together with his four other panchsiddhantika treatises, namely Paitamaha Siddhantas, Paulisha Siddhantas, Romaka Siddhantas, and Vasishta Siddhantas. The Surya Siddhanta is additionally mentioned in Aryabhata's writings.

Contributions To Mathematics

The discovery of the trigonometric formulas was one among Varahamihira's mathematical accomplishments. He improved the precision of Aryabhata's sine tables. He defined the algebraic properties of zero and negative numbers, also because the properties of positive and negative numbers. He was also one among the primary mathematicians to get a variant of the Pascal's triangle. He used it to work out the way to measure binomial coefficients. Other Significant Contributions

Varahamihira also made some important observations in ecology, hydrology, and geology. His assertion that plants and termites can detect underground water is now gaining traction within the scientific community. He was a prolific writer also . He was ready to express himself during a unique style, because of his mastery of Sanskrit grammar and poetic metre. His encyclopedic knowledge and lively presentation of even the foremost dry topics, like astronomy, made him a famous figure. His treatises like Pancha Siddhantika (Five Principles), Brihatsamhita (Master Collection), and Brahjataka (Astrological work) have elevated him to an equivalent level of Astrology as Kautilya, Manu and Panini in Political Philosophy, Law, and Grammar.
